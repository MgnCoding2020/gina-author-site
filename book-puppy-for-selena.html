About the book

“Sometimes the smallest paws leave the biggest pawprints on our hearts.”

Selena has one wish: a puppy of her very own. She can already picture the walks, the cuddles, the tail wags — but her family tells her she’ll have to earn it first. So Selena gets to work, showing love and responsibility one small act at a time.

With a gentle surprise waiting at the end, A Puppy for Selena is a tender story about what it really means to care for someone — and how the love you work for is the love that lasts.

Reading level: Preschool to Kindergarten read-aloud, or early Kindergarten independent reader.

For parents and teachers

What this book is about, underneath the story

Gentle picture books work best when grown-ups have a few handles to grab onto. Here are the themes inside A Puppy for Selena, three questions to ask after reading, and a note on when this story shines.

Themes

  • Responsibility
  • Patience
  • Family love
  • Empathy
  • Trust
  • The joy of friendship

Conversation starters

  1. What are some ways we can show love and caring to our pets, family, or friends?
  2. How do you think Buttons felt when Selena chose him and gave him a home?
  3. What is something you worked hard to earn — a book, a bike, a toy?
